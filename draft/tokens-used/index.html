A more useful and accurate analysis may be as follows: tokens used will be seen as an adoption metric, and implicitly as a loyalty metric.

Believing that AI is bringing about a productivity revolution, management will see AI as an opportunity yes, but primarily as a necessity, and an existential threat.

They fear that unless everyone gets on board the AI juggernaut, the organization will be disrupted by new-fangled competitors who are reaping the insane productivity benefits that they have been convinced exist.

Hence the problem to be solved is one of adoption, and the obstacle to be overcome, the friction caused by recalcitrant, stubborn, backwards dinosaurs. And tokens used is an adoption metric, albeit one that is extremely vulnerable to Goodhart’s law.

If you don’t use any tokens, it means that you’re part of the problem and undermining the express will of management.

The worst part is that the fear of the existential threat is not necessarily entirely unfounded, on at least two grounds. Regardless of whether or not there are any significant productivity boost due to AI.

Customer management is likely to have bought into the same productivity narratives as your management, and will be looking to get a radically better deal than before. Whether or not that is possible for anyone.

And there will be no shortage of would-be competitors popping up claiming that they are in fact reaping those benefits. Whether that’s true, both in the short and long term, doesn’t matter: harm can be immediate.

It may all be mirages, but people make stupid decisions based on mirages.
